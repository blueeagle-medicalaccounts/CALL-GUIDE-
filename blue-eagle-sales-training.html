Module 2 — Foundation
Medical Billing Basics
You can't sell what you don't understand. This module gives you the product knowledge to sound like an expert.
Why This Matters Doctors will ask you technical questions. If you hesitate or say "I'll have to get back to you on that" — you lose credibility instantly. Know these fundamentals cold.
🏥

What is a Medical Bureau?

A medical bureau handles the entire billing process on behalf of a doctor's practice — submitting claims to medical aids, following up on rejections, collecting patient payments, and managing the debtor book. We are the practice's external billing and collections department.

💻

What is Elixir Live?

Elixir Live is a cloud-based medical billing system used by thousands of practices across South Africa. It handles claim submission, electronic remittance advice (eRA), medical aid switching, debtor management, and reporting. Blue Eagle specialises exclusively in this system.

💰

How Does the Practice Make Money?

A doctor sees patients, generates invoices, and submits claims to medical aids. The medical aid pays a portion. The patient pays the rest (co-payment). If claims aren't submitted correctly or followed up — that money never arrives. We make sure it does.

❌

What is a Rejected Claim?

A rejected claim is one the medical aid refuses to pay — wrong ICD-10 code, incorrect tariff, expired membership, wrong modifier. If nobody follows up and resubmits, the practice loses that revenue permanently. We catch and recover these rejections.

Module 3 — Core Skills
The Telephonic Sales Call
Structure, tone, pace, and psychology — everything that happens from the moment you dial.
The Most Important Rule in Telephonic Sales Your voice is your only tool. The doctor cannot see you, your logo, your office, or your confidence. They only hear your tone, your pace, and your words. A calm, measured voice signals competence. A rushed, nervous voice signals desperation.
😌

Tone

Calm. Warm. Certain. Not excited, not nervous, not robotic. Sound like a professional who calls practices every day — because you do.

🐢

Pace

Slower than you think. Most new salespeople rush. Slow down by 30%. Pauses are powerful — don't fill them.

👂

Listening

The best salespeople talk less. Ask one good question, then listen completely. What they say tells you exactly what to say next.

The Call Structure
5 Stages of Every Call
1
Stage 1 — 0:00 to 0:20

The Opening

Establish who you are and why you're calling in 2 sentences. End immediately with a question. Never pitch in the opening.

2
Stage 2 — 0:20 to 1:30

Discovery

Ask one calibrated question and listen. Their answer tells you everything about their pain and what matters to them. Don't rush past this stage.

3
Stage 3 — 1:30 to 2:30

Value Statement

Based on what they said — connect their pain to your solution. One specific, relevant benefit. Not a list of features. One thing that solves their problem.

4
Stage 4 — 2:30 to 3:30

Handle Resistance

They will push back. That's normal. Every objection is a request for more information. Never argue — acknowledge, empathise, redirect.

5
Stage 5 — 3:30 to 4:00

The Close

Ask for the specific next step — a meeting, a callback, an email. Never end a call without agreeing on what happens next. Even a follow-up date is a win.

The Full Opening Script
Word for Word — Memorise This
🎙 Your Opening — Say This Exactly
Good morning — this is [your name] from Blue Eagle Medical Accounts.

We specialise in medical billing and credit control, working with practices across South Africa.

I'll keep it brief — I just wanted to ask quickly:

How are you currently managing your billing and credit control —
in-house, outsourced, or a mix of both?

⏸ STOP — Say nothing. Wait for their answer.
🧠 Why This Opening Works
Line 1: Name + company establishes who you are fast.
Line 2: Simple and direct — "we specialise in medical billing and credit control" tells them immediately what you do without overcomplicating it.
Line 3: "I'll keep it brief" signals respect for their time — doctors respond to this.
Line 4: End with a question, not a pitch. The listener controls the conversation. Their answer tells you which path to follow.
🎓 Trainer — Practice Drill (5 min)
Have the salesperson read the opening script aloud 3 times. Then close the screen and say it from memory. Focus on:
• Does it sound natural or read?
• Are they rushing the last line (the question)?
• Are they pausing after the question?

The pause after the question is critical. Most new salespeople immediately add "so basically what we do is..." — this kills it. Train the pause.
Module 4 — Access
Getting Past the Gatekeeper
The receptionist is not your enemy. They are your first sale. Win them and they'll put you through. Lose them and the call ends in 10 seconds.
The Gatekeeper Mindset Treat the receptionist like a person, not an obstacle. They have influence. They tell the doctor "there's someone on the phone who sounds different from the usual cold callers." That recommendation is worth more than anything you can say to the doctor directly.
✅

What Works

Warmth. Calm confidence. Treating them with respect. Asking their name. Saying please and thank you genuinely. Sounding like you call practices every day — because you do.

❌

What Kills It

Sounding like you're reading a script. Being pushy when they say the doctor is busy. Using corporate jargon. Asking "is the doctor available?" as the very first thing you say.

You've removed the pressure completely. You're not asking for the doctor's time — you're asking for an email address. Almost no one refuses that. Now you have a warm email going to the right inbox.

Module 5 — Resilience
Objection Handling
Every objection is a question in disguise. Your job is to understand what they're really asking — and answer that.
The Golden Rule of Objections Never argue. Never defend. Never explain at length. The formula is always: Acknowledge → Empathise → Redirect. Three steps, every time. An objection means they're still on the phone — that's actually a good sign.
🔁

The AER Formula

A
Acknowledge
Show you heard them. Don't dismiss. Don't immediately counter.
E
Empathise
Validate their concern. Make them feel understood before redirecting.
R
Redirect
Ask a question that moves the conversation forward. Never a statement.
The 8 Objections
Complete Response Guide

Objection 1
"We already have a billing company."
▼
🎙 Response
No problem at all — most practices we work with actually had another service provider before speaking to us.

Out of curiosity, are you completely happy with the communication and reporting you're currently getting?

⏸ Wait — don't add anything
🧠 The Move
Never attack the current provider. Validate their choice first. The calibrated question almost always reveals a crack — communication, reporting, or follow-up. If they say completely happy → offer to keep details on file. If they hesitate → dig deeper.
Objection 2
"We're happy with our current setup."
▼
🎙 Response
That's great — sounds like they're doing something right.

⏸ 2 second pause

Do you currently receive a monthly report showing your total rejected claims and outstanding debt?

⏸ Listen
🧠 The Move
The reporting question is a Black Swan probe. Most practices don't get proper monthly reports. If they hesitate or say no — that's your opening. "That's actually where most of the money gets missed — we build that into every client from day one."
Objection 3
"We don't use Elixir Live."
▼
🎙 Response
That's fine — a number of practices we've onboarded have moved across from other systems.

We handle the migration completely — no disruption to the practice at all.

The reason we specialise exclusively in Elixir Live is that we can operate at a much deeper level on one platform than a bureau juggling five different systems.

What's your biggest frustration with your current billing setup?
🧠 The Move
Handle the system objection quickly — don't dwell on it. Immediately pivot to their pain. The question at the end is your discovery question — their answer tells you exactly what matters to them.
Objection 4
"How much do you charge?"
▼
🎙 Response
Our pricing depends on the practice setup.

Most practices fall into one of two structures — either a 5% on collections model, so if nothing is collected there's no fee, or a fixed monthly arrangement depending on volume.

What we prefer doing first is understanding the practice properly and then proposing something fair for both sides.

Would a short 15-minute review make sense as a starting point?
🧠 The Move
Don't give a hard number immediately — you haven't seen their practice yet. The 5% alignment frame is powerful: "if nothing is collected, there's no fee" removes their financial risk perception. End with the meeting close — this is the moment to ask.
Objection 5
"Too expensive / your fee is too high."
▼
🎙 Response
I hear you — but think of it this way. If we recover R30,000 to R80,000 a month that you're currently not collecting, you're actually making money, not spending it. The fee covers itself.

How much is currently sitting outstanding in your accounts right now?

⏸ Wait for their answer
🧠 The Move
Reframe the fee as an investment with a specific return. The R30K–R80K figure is a loss anchor — they're now thinking about money they're already losing. The final question forces them to imagine the benefit in their own reality. That's where decisions get made.
Objection 6
"I don't have time right now."
▼
🎙 Response
I completely understand and I'll respect your time.

When would genuinely be a better moment — later this week, or early next week?

⏸ Wait for a day

If they still push back:

No problem at all — can I at least send through a short overview email for when you have two minutes?
🧠 The Move
Never end a call without a next step. The binary choice (this week / next week) assumes the conversation continues and only asks when. The email fallback keeps the door open even if they won't commit to a call.
Objection 7
"Switching would be too much hassle."
▼
🎙 Response
It sounds like the transition itself is the main concern — and honestly, that's the most common thing we hear.

We handle the entire onboarding process. Most practices are fully set up within 5 to 7 working days with zero disruption to their workflow.

What would the transition need to look like for it to feel manageable for your practice?
🧠 The Move
Label their fear ("the transition itself is the main concern") then immediately neutralise it with specifics. The final calibrated question invites them to design the solution — when people design it themselves, they own it and resist it less.
Objection 8
"Send me info and I'll think about it."
▼
🎙 Response
Of course — what's the best email address?

⏸ Get email immediately

And just so I send the most relevant information — what would be most useful: the collections and follow-up process, the reporting, or the rejected claims recovery side?

⏸ Their answer = their hot button

Perfect. And would it be a bad idea if I followed up later this week once you've had a chance to glance at it?
🧠 The Move
Get the email before anything else. Then find their priority — use it as your follow-up hook. The final No-Oriented question ("would it be a bad idea...") gets permission for a follow-up call without them feeling pressured.
🎓 Trainer — Objection Drill (5 min)
Fire these objections at the salesperson in random order. They must respond immediately — no pausing to think. If they hesitate or get it wrong, repeat that objection until it's automatic.

Speed is the goal. In a real call there's no time to think. The response must be muscle memory.
Module 6 — The Critical Skill
Closing & Booking Meetings
This is where most salespeople fail. Not because the doctor said no — but because the salesperson never actually asked.
The Biggest Mistake in Medical Billing Sales Having a great conversation, building rapport, finding the pain — and then ending the call with "I'll send you some info." That's not a close. That's a polite goodbye. A close means asking for a specific commitment. Every time.
❌

Weak Close

"I'll send you some information and you can have a look when you get a chance."

This puts them in control with no commitment. 90% of these never respond.

✅

Strong Close

"What works better — earlier in the week or towards the end of the week?"

This assumes the meeting is happening. They're choosing when, not whether.

The Close That Works
The Ageing Report Review Close
Your most powerful close. Offer something of real value with zero risk to them.
🎙 The Full Close — Memorise This
Doctor, based on what you've mentioned —
[reflect their specific pain back]

— it sounds like there may be room to recover some revenue that's currently slipping through.

What would make the most sense is a short 15–20 minute review where we look at your current billing position together on Elixir Live.

We'll look at your ageing report, identify any obvious collection gaps, and you'll walk away with a clearer picture — no obligation at all.

What works better — earlier in the week, or towards the end of the week?

⏸ STOP. Say nothing. Let them pick a day.
🧠 Why Every Line Matters
"Based on what you've mentioned" — This creates the "That's right" moment. Summarising their pain back to them makes them feel heard and understood. They almost always agree.

"No obligation at all" — Removes the fear. They're not committing to anything by saying yes to a meeting.

"Earlier in the week or towards the end?" — The binary choice. Never ask "would you like to meet?" — that's an easy no. The binary assumes yes and just asks when. This is the single most effective close in consultative sales.

🎓 Trainer — Close Drill (5 min)
Run this scenario with the salesperson:

You play the doctor. Say: "Things have been a bit of an issue with our current bureau actually — communication is really poor."

The salesperson must:
1. Label the pain ("It sounds like...")
2. Validate ("That's actually very common...")
3. Deliver the full close script from memory
4. Say nothing after the binary question

Common mistake: They add "or I can send you information instead" after the binary question. This gives the doctor an easy exit. Train them to stop talking the moment the question is out.
